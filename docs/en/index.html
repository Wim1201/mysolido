The problem

Your data isn't with you

Personal documents, medical records, photos and account details are scattered across servers of American tech companies. You have no control over who can access them.

No ownership

Google, Microsoft and Apple set the terms. They own the servers where your life is stored.

No transparency

You don't know who views your data, how long it's stored, or what it's used for.

No control

The CLOUD Act gives the US government access to data on American servers — regardless of where you live.

The solution

Install your own data vault

MySolido runs on your PC. Your documents never leave your computer unless you choose to share them. You decide who gets access and for how long.

  • Local storage — Everything stays on your own hard drive
  • You control access — Share specific files with specific people
  • Open standard — Built on W3C Solid, no vendor lock-in
  • No subscription — Download once, yours forever

Security

Three layers of protection when sharing

When you share data via MySolido, you are protected on three levels.

Technical

Built-in measures that prevent unauthorised use.

  • Watermarks on shared files
  • Traceable to the recipient
  • No download button for recipients
  • Temporary links with expiry date

Legal

Machine-readable rules that are legally enforceable.

  • ODRL 2.2 policies per folder
  • No reselling, no copying
  • Rules travel with the data
  • W3C standard, no vendor lock-in

Registration

Every consent is recorded as proof.

  • ISO 27560 consent records
  • W3C Data Privacy Vocabulary
  • Revocable with one click
  • Full audit trail
